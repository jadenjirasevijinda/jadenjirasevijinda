Editing in DaVinci Resolve

YouTube Upload Workflow
Video· Beginner
›

Export at 1080p or 4K — H.264 or H.265, high bitrate for YouTube's compression.
2
Write your title & description — include keywords naturally, add timestamps.
3
Design a thumbnail — high contrast, readable text, bold face or object in frame.

03
Photography Basics
Photo· Beginner
›
1
Understand the exposure triangle — ISO, Shutter Speed, and Aperture work together.
2
Shoot in RAW — gives you far more flexibility when editing in Lightroom.
3
Use the rule of thirds — place your subject off-center for a more dynamic composition.
4
Focus carefully — use single-point AF for portraits, continuous AF for moving subjects.
5
Edit in Lightroom — adjust exposure, then whites/blacks, then color, then sharpness last.
04
Color Grading in Lightroom
Photo· Intermediate
›
1
Start with a neutral base — set white balance, then bring exposure to a natural level.
2
Use the tone curve — create an S-curve for contrast; lift blacks for a matte look.
3
Adjust HSL — shift hue of specific colors (e.g. make greens more teal, skin more golden).
4
Color grading panel — add a color cast to shadows, midtones, or highlights for mood.
5
Save as a preset — sync across your whole shoot with one click.

05
Recording Audio with Rodecaster Pro
Audio· Beginner
›
1
Connect your mic — XLR into Channel 1, enable phantom power if condenser mic.
2
Set gain — speak at normal volume, aim for peaks around -12 to -6 dB.
3
Enable APHEX processing — Aural Exciter and Big Bottom give your voice warmth and clarity.
4
Record via USB — select the Rodecaster as your audio input in DaVinci or your DAW.
5
Monitor with headphones — always track with cans on so you catch any issues live.
